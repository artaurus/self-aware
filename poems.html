Divine Footprints




He was the Lord's devotee staunch,
Had unwavering faith & sublime devotion;
God alone, he did see & munch,
In & thru' every motion;

Every morn he walked the beach,
Never alone but with his God;
Four footprints then, two of each,
Trailed on the wet sands of the Lord;

Yet things at work went sour,
And difficult times entered:
“Help me thru' this hour;”
Was his fervent plea, God-centered;

He went to the beach as usual,
But now saw just two footprints trail:
“God, I am but a frail individual,
Without you, my train will derail!”

His faith shook, but did not break,
And he left to his God his shaky lake;
Ripples then slept to calm's wake,
And his present then did not bake;

So at the beach then, next morn,
He saw four footprints again trail:
“God, I was so mentally torn,
But you left me to lonesome sail!”

God smiled at him & spoke divine:
“The footprints which you saw as yours,
They weren't yours, but mine;
I was carrying you, & all things yours;

When all is well, I walk with you;
When you are unwell, I carry you;
Never ever am I not with you;
We are as one, I & you.”

The Drop and the Sea




Just after it had heavily rained,
Which washed all trees & weeds so clean,
The sand was wet, & unstained,
The sea was calm with light waves seen;

On a tree's leaf, lively & green,
Facing the sky-blue sea endless,
A drop sparkled clear & clean,
It too was meant to be, no less;

It was but a drop tiny,
In this vast & lasting universe;
Yet its existence was shiny,
With an equally wondrous verse;

It had just been divinely formed,
And ere long it would not ever be;
Yet it was not at all alarmed,
It knew it was no less than the sea;

Then the leaf a breeze did scrape,
And the drop fine danced in delight;
It stretched, & then regained its shape,
Crystal clear, it balanced light;

It could see the mighty sea,
Beyond the beach, & waving tame;
And knew its oneness with the sea:
Water in a drop & sea is the same;

The drop was tiny, with brief a life,
The sea was vast & long lasting;
Though they differed in form & strife,
The same essence, water, was all-casting;

So cheery & merry, the drop couldn’t stop,
Identifying with self as 'water', not 'drop';
Knowing its essence was the same as sea,
It had no otherness, or differences to see;

Likewise for the tiny & brief individual,
In this universe vast & lasting;
Differences appear in forms plural,
But the same Essence, Self, is all-casting;

Being amidst the world of plural,
Identifying only with 'Self', not 'individual',
And knowing Essence-Self is same in all,
It's oneness without otherness with all.

Enlighten Yourself




In the Divine Plan universal,
Is Earth, with living beings global;
Of which the human being classical,
Can perceive, feel, & think, able;

Man can turn a heaven into hell,
When unkind thoughts & actions swell;
Or can turn a hell into heaven,
When kind thoughts & actions pen;

Heaven & hell are not geographical,
They are states of one's own mind;
A disturbed & fearful mind is hell,
Divine love in mind is heavenly kind;

In harmonic times, healthily helpful,
Chirping & roving & merrily free;
But picking up dust, Self-forgetful,
From dust to dust, it leads silently;

Enter pandemic times, harmfully viral,
Quarantined by hellish limits & fear;
Clean & clear, with the Self nondual,
And to enlighten, spiritually steer;

The Self is Consciousness, all-enlivening;
The Self is everywhere, all-pervading;
The Self is indivisible, without a part;
The Self is full, lacks nothing at Heart;

Ignorant of own Supreme Self State,
Man leaves peace & bliss for worldly slot;
With repeated birth & death, as fate,
Has stories of that which truly is not!

From vasana, ignorance of the Self,
As the causal body, beyond thought level,
A subtle body rises, mind-intellect of self,
And forms a physical gross body, to dwell;

One then identifies not with the Self,
But identifies with the bodies in reel;
With egoic attitudes of an illusory elf,
Has desires to fulfill, & happy feel;

Then, at individual, or collective a level,
Own mind becomes a heaven or hell;
Hell is a mind agitating you viral,
Heaven is when in divine love you dwell;

Like, coronavirus in mind is a fearful hell,
With dis-ease, decay, & death's swell;
In virus-free divine love's heaven to dwell,
Real-ise the Self, & live liberated well;

Thou art the Self-Atman-Brahman;
Declared the Masters; O Eternal One!
Know your Real Self, & nondual run,
Other than You, the Self, there's none;

In oneness with all, as Self own,
With divine love and kindness sown;
Yet beyond terrestrial kiss & hiss,
Self-aware, in eternal peace & bliss!

Perceive the Self, O Sun of Suns!
Feel the Self, the Consciousness!
Think as the Self, O Glorious Ones!
And be in unbroken happiness.
