The Drop and the Sea


Just after it had heavily rained,
Which washed all trees & weeds so clean,
The sand was wet, & unstained,
The sea was calm with light waves seen;

On a tree's leaf, lively & green,
Facing the sky-blue sea endless,
A drop sparkled clear & clean,
It too was meant to be, no less;

It was but a drop tiny,
In this vast & lasting universe;
Yet its existence was shiny,
With an equally wondrous verse;

It had just been divinely formed,
And ere long it would not ever be;
Yet it was not at all alarmed,
It knew it was no less than the sea;

Then the leaf a breeze did scrape,
And the drop fine danced in delight;
It stretched, & then regained its shape,
Crystal clear, it balanced light;

It could see the mighty sea,
Beyond the beach, & waving tame;
And knew its oneness with the sea:
Water in a drop & sea is the same;

The drop was tiny, with brief a life,
The sea was vast & long lasting;
Though they differed in form & strife,
The same essence, water, was all-casting;

Identifying with self as 'water', not 'drop';
Knowing its essence was the same as sea,
It had no otherness, or differences to see;

Likewise for the tiny & brief individual,
In this universe vast & lasting;
Differences appear in forms plural,
But the same Essence, Self, is all-casting;

Being amidst the world of plural,
Identifying only with 'Self', not 'individual',
And knowing Essence-Self is same in all,
It's oneness without otherness with all.
