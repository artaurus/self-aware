Vedanta


Veda means Knowledge, and anta means end. Vedanta, literally meaning End of Knowledge, is the sublime philosophy of ancient India. It gives the purpose and principles of life and living, enables one to think original, choose the right course of action, and act on it. And above all, it leads to your Real Self.


Timeless, Vedanta is eternal; it always was, is, and ever will be. Limitless, Vedanta is universal; in every star, rock, brook, tree, animal, and human being. Flawless, Vedanta stands on its own Truth; beyond race, creed, religion, and authority.


Along with growth and fleeting joys, life also offers challenges, mental dis-ease and physical decay, besides inevitable death. And individuals unnecessarily experience harmful negatives like agitations, stress and strain. Not lasting peace and happiness.


Practical Vedanta develops the intellect, purifies the mind and enables actions in the spirit of service and sacrifice. Its technique of living uplifts above harmful negatives to divine thought, chaste emotions, unselfish actions, and a clean environment. Enhancing peace and prosperity in society.


And the philosophy of Vedanta points to the Self. Leading a sincere and devoted seeker to the eternal peace and unbroken bliss of Self-realization, God-realization, and reaching the Ultimate State of Being, Enlightenment.
