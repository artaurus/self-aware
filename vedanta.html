Vedanta


Veda means Knowledge, and anta means end. Vedanta, literally meaning End of Knowledge, is the sublime philosophy of ancient India. It gives the purpose and principles of life and living, enables one to think original, choose the right course of action, and act on it.


  • Timeless, Vedanta is eternal; it always was, is, and ever will be.
  • Limitless, Vedanta is universal; in every star, rock, brook, tree, animal, and human being.
  • Flawless, Vedanta stands on its own Truth; beyond race, creed, religion, and authority.

Along with birth, growth and joys, life also offers the sorrows of dis-ease, decay and death. Individuals then unnecessarily experience negatives like mental stress, physical ailments, and discord in relationships. Not lasting peace and happiness.


Practical Vedanta develops the intellect and purifies the mind. Enabling divine thought, chaste emotions, selfless actions and clean environment. Enhancing peace and prosperity, happiness and success, in society.


And the philosophy of Vedanta points to the Transcendental Self. By which seekers can gain absolute fulfillment, eternal peace and unbroken bliss in Self-realization, God-realization, and reach the ultimate State of Being.
